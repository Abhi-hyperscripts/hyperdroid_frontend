What is Professional Tax (PT)?

Professional Tax is a state-level tax levied on individuals earning income through employment, profession, or trade. It is collected by state governments in India and is deducted from the employee's salary by the employer.

The maximum Professional Tax that can be levied is ₹2,500 per year as per the Constitution of India. Different states have different PT slabs and rules.

PT Deduction under Income Tax

Professional Tax paid is fully deductible from your gross salary while calculating taxable income under Section 16(iii) of the Income Tax Act. This means PT reduces your tax liability.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the maximum Professional Tax in India?
As per Article 276 of the Constitution of India, the maximum Professional Tax that can be levied is ₹2,500 per year. No state can charge more than this limit.
Who is liable to pay Professional Tax?
Professional Tax is applicable to salaried employees, self-employed professionals, and traders in states where PT is levied. For salaried employees, the employer deducts PT from salary and deposits it with the state government.
Is Professional Tax deductible for income tax?
Yes, Professional Tax paid is fully deductible from your gross salary under Section 16(iii) of the Income Tax Act. This deduction is available under both old and new tax regimes.
What happens if PT is not deducted?
If an employer fails to deduct and deposit PT, they may face penalties including interest on delayed payment. Employers are responsible for compliance and timely deposit of PT with the state government.
