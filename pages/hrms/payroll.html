How Arrears Work - Click to Learn

What are Arrears?

Arrears are the difference in salary when an employee's pay is revised with a past effective date. If payroll was already processed at the old salary, arrears compensate for the difference.

Two Types of Arrears

Version Arrears

When a Salary Structure is updated (e.g., HRA % increased) and applied to past periods.

Example: Company increases HRA from 40% to 50% for all employees effective Jan 1st.
CTC Revision Arrears

When an individual employee's CTC is revised (e.g., promotion, annual increment).

Example: Employee gets promotion with CTC increase from ₹18L to ₹20L effective Jan 1st.

Step-by-Step: CTC Revision Arrears Flow

1
Payroll Processed

January payroll is processed at employee's current CTC (₹18L)

Jan Salary: ₹1,50,000
2
Salary Revised (Retroactive)

In February, HR revises CTC to ₹19.2L with effective date Jan 1st

Go to: Employee Salaries → Click "Revise"
3
Pending Calculation Appears

System detects that January was paid at old CTC. A "Pending Arrears Calculations" alert appears.

Go to: Arrears → CTC Revision Arrears
4
Calculate Arrears

Click "Calculate Arrears" button. System computes the difference.

New Monthly: ₹1,60,000
Old Monthly: - ₹1,50,000
Arrears: = ₹10,000
5
Review & Apply

Arrears appear in the table with "pending" status. You can:

✓ Apply - Add to next payroll ✕ Cancel - Don't pay arrears 👁 View - See breakdown
6
Arrears Paid

In February payroll, employee receives:

Feb Salary: ₹1,60,000
Jan Arrears: + ₹10,000
Total: = ₹1,70,000

Quick Tips

Always process regular payroll before revising salaries retroactively
Check "Pending Arrears Calculations" section after any salary revision
Arrears are automatically calculated for all affected months
Use "Bulk Apply" to apply multiple arrears at once
